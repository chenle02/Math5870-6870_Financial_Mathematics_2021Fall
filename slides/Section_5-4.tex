\begin{mydefinition}
	\textcolor{magenta}{\bf Future contracts} are essentially exchange-traded forward contracts.
\end{mydefinition}
\bigskip

Typical features of futures contracts include:

\begin{itemize}
	\item Standardized, with specified delivery dates, locations, procedures
	\item A clearinghouse
	\item[] Matches buy and sell orders
	\item[] Keeps track of membersâ€™ obligations and payments
	\item[] After matching the trades, becomes counterparty
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
%-------------- end slide -------------------------------%}}}
%-------------- start slide -------------------------------%{{{ 1
\begin{frame}[fragile,t]
Differences from forward contracts
\begin{itemize}
	\item Settled daily through the \textcolor{magenta}{mark-to-market} process
	\item Highly liquid: easier to offset an existing position
	\item Highly standardized structure
	\item Low credit risk
	\item There are typically daily price limits.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
